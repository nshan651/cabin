Factory Farming

Notes

  • Most people, especially those in modern urban life, only come into contact with non-human animals during our meals
  • Factory farming stats (per year):
    • 100 million cows, pigs, and sheep raised and slaughtered
    • 5 billion chickens
  • Buying packaged food gives us a clinical, sanatized, and (mostly) bloodless way of consuming animal products
  • Origin of “meat”: any solid food, not necessarily limited to animal flesh
    • The word has evolved over time, similarly to how cereals in America are now mostly associated with the sugary breakfast food.
  • Idyllicism of the small, family-owned farm does not track with the reality of the coldness of industrial farming
  • Factory farms began their reign of control by taking over poultry production some 50 years ago
  • Today, 50 large corporations control virtually all poultry production
  • Companies with no previous experience or interest in agribusiness pivoted to farming at scale in order to diversify and to take advantage of tax concessions:
    • For these companies, farming is a calculated economic decision
